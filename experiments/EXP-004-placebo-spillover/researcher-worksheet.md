## The question on the table

EXP-002 found that placebo signals (published, but no predictability claim) drift down slightly after publication: −0.11 %/mo. EXP-003 showed calendar-era controls can't cleanly kill or confirm that drift. The leading suspect is **arbitrage spillover**: many placebos are correlated with real published predictors (they're built from the same accounting ingredients), so capital trading the real predictors may drag the correlated placebos along.

The proposed test: measure each placebo's correlation with the published-predictor universe, and check whether more-correlated placebos decay more after publication.
