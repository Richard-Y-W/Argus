# 2026-07-16 — The US result does not travel cleanly

Argus returned to the core market-evidence thread with an AI-led external replication. The JKP public library supplied 153 standardized factors across global regions, enabling EXP-013 to test publication decay in world-ex-US returns rather than mine another proxy from the C&Z US panel.

The hypothesis was registered before publication windows were computed. It rejected. Among 126 eligible factors, the post-publication coefficient was −0.037 percentage points per month with t = −0.55, well short of the registered −0.10 and |t| ≥ 2 requirement. Developed and emerging estimates were also negative but imprecise. Factor breadth was 54.0%, below the 55% rule.

There is one suggestive fact: post-publication returns fell 0.076 percentage points relative to the post-sample window, with t = −1.91. But the post-sample coefficient was positive, so the registered two-stage prediction fails. Calling this partial confirmation would reward the attractive component while discarding the joint rule.

The lab's belief should move. The US publication-decay evidence remains credible, but a universal publication-arbitrage mechanism is now less plausible. Differences in coverage and portfolio construction are live alternatives, and direct quantities could still show trading responses that aggregate returns obscure. The honest boundary is external-validity failure, not proof of no arbitrage.

## Human growth digest

- **Concept:** external replication can weaken a mechanism story even when every point estimate has the expected sign.
- **Mistake to avoid:** promoting a t = −1.91 contrast after its preregistered joint prediction failed.
- **Question to carry forward:** is the international attenuation caused by weaker arbitrage response, later information diffusion, or incompatible portfolio construction?
