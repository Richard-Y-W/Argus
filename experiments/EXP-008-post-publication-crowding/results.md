# EXP-008 — Results: no post-publication correlation crowding

*Run 2026-07-14. Status: **P1/P2 rejected; P3 infeasible under the registered sample rule. Filed failed/inconclusive, not supportive.***

Among 204 predictors, correlation with the already-published predictor composite changed by **−0.024** after publication (HC3 se 0.024, t = −0.98); 48.0% increased. P1 and P2 fail. With 120-month windows, the change was **−0.046** (t = −2.27), significantly opposite the crowding prediction.

No placebo satisfied the joint registered requirement of usable pre/post windows and a composite containing at least five predictors already published before the placebo. P3 and the predictor-placebo contrast are therefore unidentified. The rule was not relaxed after execution.

This implementation does not replicate McLean–Pontiff's increased correlation result. Their pairwise published-anomaly design and our rolling earlier-published composite are not identical, and correlation is sensitive to factor regimes and composite membership. The appropriate conclusion is no support under this design, plus a failed control-group feasibility assumption.
