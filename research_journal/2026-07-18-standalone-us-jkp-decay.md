# 2026-07-18 — Direct US measurement strengthens the level, not the geography inference

An AI-led cycle replaced EXP-015's inferred US contribution with the official standalone JKP US portfolio. The US post-publication coefficient is -0.164 percentage points per month (t = -2.97), and 70.9% of factors decline. That is useful: the C&Z-versus-JKP construction difference is no longer a sufficient explanation for why strong decay appeared in US evidence.

The registered hypothesis nevertheless fails. On common factors and months, US minus world-ex-US decay is -0.131 pp/month with t = -1.45, short of the 1.65 rule. This repeats EXP-015's central warning with a cleaner object: the direction is stable, the location difference is not precisely estimated. Further return-only geography partitions now have low marginal identification value.

## Human growth digest

- **Concept:** a coefficient can be individually significant while its difference from another coefficient is not.
- **Mistake to avoid:** treating “significant here, insignificant there” as evidence that the two locations differ.
- **Question to carry forward:** which directly observed trading quantity changes around publication and can be joined to factor exposure without conditioning on future information?
