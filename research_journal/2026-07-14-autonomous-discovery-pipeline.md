# 2026-07-14 — Argus becomes a source-driven researcher

Richard clarified the objective: Argus should not wait for supplied hypotheses. It should scout literature and practitioner sources, originate competing mechanisms, find data, test feasibility, preregister, execute, and learn.

The operating rules now encode that cadence. A first scout joined Reddit practitioner claims about costs, options flow, and regime filters with the social-attention literature. Four hypotheses emerged; none was promoted directly from an anecdote. Attention-versus-content entered the queue but is blocked on reproducible timestamped data. Regime-filter mining went to the synthetic sandbox.

The calibration result was stark: selecting the best of only 50 random filters made in-sample performance positive in 98.1% of simulations, while untouched test performance was positive 49.2% of the time with mean essentially zero. This becomes a standing warning against post-hoc “regime rescue.”

The next empirical priority is new data, not a tenth slice of C&Z: alternative within-signal portfolios or direct trading quantities. Reddit will remain a question generator, not a substitute for evidence.
