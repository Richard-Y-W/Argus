# 2026-07-14 — Autonomy, arbitrage lineage, and a failed spillover proxy

Richard removed the researcher-workbook constraint: he is busy and wants the lab to keep testing. The charter and operating rules now make participation optional, never a gate. Attribution remains strict, so autonomous output is AI-led rather than retroactively credited to him.

The repository gained a real sandbox boundary: fast probes are exploratory, synthetic tests verify shared statistics, and only a Git-registered experiment can support a claim. The first infrastructure test caught an import-path defect; EXP-004 then caught a subtler cross-file date defect because predictor and placebo releases encoded months differently. Both are fixed explicitly.

The new publication-arbitrage lineage decomposes the story from costly information and limits to arbitrage through data mining, the factor zoo, McLean–Pontiff's three windows, and Argus's control tests. Its main point is that “the market digests the edge” is six links, not one slogan.

EXP-004 tested one missing link: return spillover into correlated placebos. The raw high-versus-low exposure difference looked strong, but it was another scale/composition trap. The controlled exposure coefficient was effectively zero; future predictors also “explained” the outcome; and a composite exposure had the wrong sign. Return correlation is too ambiguous to proxy actual capital transmission. The next arbitrage work should move toward quantities—short interest, turnover, holdings, lending costs, or security-level overlap.
