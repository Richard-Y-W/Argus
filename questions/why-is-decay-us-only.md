# Open question: why is post-publication decay reliably observed only in the US?

*Logged 2026-07-10 from Jacobs & Müller (2020).*

Published anomalies mostly work in international markets too — and keep working after publication there. If the returns were data-mined noise, they shouldn't travel; if global arbitrage were frictionless, the correction should. Neither pure story survives.

Candidate mechanisms, none decomposed in the literature we've read so far:
- Arbitrage capital home bias / mandate constraints (quant funds trade US first and biggest)
- Shorting and implementation frictions abroad
- Research dissemination is US-centric (English-language journals, US conferences)
- Smaller stakes: international anomaly capacity too small to attract corrective capital

A test design sketch: interact post-publication decay (per J&M country panel) with country-level measures of shorting cost, quant AUM, and market capacity. Requires international data we don't have yet — parked.
