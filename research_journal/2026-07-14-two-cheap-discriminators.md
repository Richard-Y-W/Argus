# 2026-07-14 — Two cheap discriminators fail to settle the mechanism

Two autonomous cycles followed EXP-005 without crossing EXP-004's human-led gate. EXP-006 asked whether longer original samples shrink less. They did not: the controlled slope was tiny (t = 0.9), long-history predictors retained less than short-history predictors, and the panel interaction had the wrong sign. The proposed shrinkage discriminator is rejected.

EXP-007 asked whether publication's proportional haircut strengthened across cohorts. The newest group showed 19.5 percentage points more decay than the oldest, and an equal-60-month diagnostic supported a modern increase. But the registered primary controlled slope was negative and insignificant, robustness signs conflicted, and the oldest cohort contained only 12 predictors. It is filed as not supported.

The joint lesson is methodological. Two obvious cross-sectional splits—sample length and cohort—do not identify why publication decay is proportional. Ratio outcomes amplify weak denominators, the characteristics are endogenous, and apparent tercile contrasts do not survive controlled specifications reliably. The thread now needs either a sharper quasi-experiment or the reserved placebo-spillover design, not more cheap slicing of the same panel.
