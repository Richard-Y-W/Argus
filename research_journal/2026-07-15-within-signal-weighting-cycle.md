# 2026-07-15 — Pairing removes a confound, not the uncertainty

The lab completed an AI-led cycle on the next declared priority: whether value-weighted versions of the same published signals decay more than equal-weighted versions. The official October 2025 alternative decile portfolios made the comparison possible without relying on original authors' EW/VW choices.

EXP-010 was registered before execution. It paired signals and months, fixed a positive-signal threshold to stabilize proportional decay, included a levels difference-in-differences, measured breadth, and registered winsorized and clear-only checks. The primary mean was economically nontrivial (+0.141 more proportional decay under VW) but missed its inference rule (t = 1.76). The levels test pointed the wrong way, exactly half the signals favored VW decay, and the median difference was only +0.021. HYP-010 is rejected.

This is useful closure on EXP-009. Its dramatic across-signal raw gap does not survive as a broad paired fact. The experiment does not prove EW and VW decay equally; its interval still admits material differences. It does show that portfolio weighting is too unstable and indirect to carry the arbitrage-mechanism claim.

The main statistical lesson for Richard is the distinction between mean magnitude and evidence breadth. A mean can clear an economic threshold while a 95% interval crosses zero, the median sits near zero, and half the units have the opposite sign. Robustness that becomes significant after winsorization is informative about tails, but it cannot replace a preregistered primary result.

The next high-value move is away from another proxy mined from the same return panel. Direct trading quantities or a second market/data family offer more identification value.

## Human growth digest

- **Concept:** a paired design removes time-invariant between-signal composition, but it does not make weighting a causal intervention.
- **Mistake to avoid:** calling a directionally correct t = 1.76 result “marginally supported” when the registered rule says reject.
- **Question to carry forward:** what observable quantity would change specifically when arbitrage capital enters, rather than merely co-move with signal returns?
