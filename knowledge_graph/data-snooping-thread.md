# Data-snooping and holdout thread

White (2000) establishes the inference problem created by specification search. Hansen (2005) refines superior-predictive-ability testing, and Bailey et al. (2016) applies the overfitting problem to repeated financial-strategy design.

The Argus regime-filter sandbox supplied the initial demonstration at K=50. EXP-011 registered the search-breadth gradient and found that naive significance rises from 5.36% at one null candidate to 99.99% at 200, without test persistence. EXP-012 then registered a governance test: requiring an untouched second confirmation reduced idealized null false claims from 5.145% to 0.274%.

The live boundary is clear. These simulations establish behavior under independent Gaussian null statistics. They do not estimate real-market false-discovery rates, solve dependent multiple testing, measure power, or demonstrate a profitable regime filter.
